# Conference Abstract: The Science of Being Hopeful

**Session Title:** The Science of Being Hopeful
**Speaker:** Brian Hackerson, Co-Creator, Agile Best Self
**Format:** Keynote / Breakout (30–45 min)

---

## Abstract

Most people treat hope like the weather — something that either shows up or doesn't. But research tells a different story. Hope isn't a feeling. It's a practice. And it can be built.

Drawing on C.R. Snyder's Hope Theory, neuroscience, and hard-won personal experience, this session reframes hope as a discipline — one that becomes most essential precisely when life gets hardest. Participants will explore why optimism and hope are not the same thing, how the inner critic actually works (and why arguing with it makes things worse), and what it takes to build a hope practice that holds up under real adversity.

Attendees will leave with a working definition of hope, a practical framework built around four pillars, and one concrete first step they can take before tomorrow morning.

*Hope is not something you find. It's something you build.*

---

## Key Takeaways

- Hope is a practice, not a personality trait — and it can be deliberately built
- The difference between optimism and hope, and why it matters under pressure
- How the inner critic works and what hope actually does to it
- A four-pillar framework for building a personal hope practice
- One concrete first step: finding your keyword
