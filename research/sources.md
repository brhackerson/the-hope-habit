## Books

### Snyder, C.R.
**The Psychology of Hope: You Can Get There from Here**
Free Press, 1994
- Core framework: Hope Theory — agency (goal-directed energy and belief you can move forward) + pathways (ability to find or create routes when blocked)
- Key insight: Hope is cognitive, not emotional. Both agency and pathways require action.
- Hopeful people are more clear-eyed about obstacles than pure optimists — they are already looking for alternative routes
- Includes the Hope Scale — a self-assessment tool for measuring hope
- Snyder wrote the book during a difficult personal period involving multiple surgeries and hospitalizations
- Referenced by Brené Brown in *The Gifts of Imperfection*
- Available: Amazon, Target, Barnes & Noble; ebook via Everand; no audiobook as of early 2026
- **Status:** To be read by Brian

## Books Partially Reviewed by Brian

### Duckworth, Angela
**Grit: The Power of Passion and Perseverance**
Scribner, 2016
- Brian has seen the TED talk (one of the most watched ever) but has not read the book yet
- Key concepts: sustained effort toward meaningful goals, passion + perseverance as predictors of success over talent/intelligence
- **Differentiation notes:**
  - Grit frames perseverance as a personality trait you have or develop — hope practice frames hope as a system you design iteratively
  - Duckworth is prescriptive ("here's what grit is, here's how to build it") — Brian's posture is humble and invitational ("here's my practice, go build yours")
  - Grit emphasizes staying the course — hope practice embraces pivoting, adjusting, and course correction (Principle 12)
  - Snyder's agency + pathways framework is mechanistically distinct from grit
  - Different life stage — Brian's story resonates with a different audience than Duckworth's typical examples
- **Status:** TED talk viewed ✓ — book to be read before manuscript is complete

### Sinek, Simon
**Start With Why: How Great Leaders Inspire Everyone to Take Action**
Portfolio/Penguin, 2009
- Brian has read this book ✓
- Brian met Simon Sinek and spoke with him about work being done at 3M
- Key concepts relevant to the book:
  - The Golden Circle — why, how, what — purpose drives everything
  - People don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it
  - The why as the foundation of sustainable motivation
  - Directly connects to the keyword mechanism and "Pictures" concept — the hope practice is built on a compelling why
- **Status:** Read by Brian ✓

### Sinek, Simon
**The Infinite Game**
Portfolio/Penguin, 2019
- Brian has read this book ✓
- Key concepts relevant to the book:
  - Infinite mindset — playing not to win but to keep playing
  - Advancing a just cause — a purpose worth sacrificing for
  - Resilience over short-term wins
  - Hope is fundamentally an infinite game — not a finish line but a sustaining practice
  - Breaking 80, walking the course in retirement, being present in the pictures across generations — all infinite game goals
  - Strong parallel to the hope practice framework — you don't finish hope, you keep building it
- **Status:** Read by Brian ✓

## Frameworks & Systems

### Hackerson, Brian and Gardner, Michaele
**Agile Best Self — 12 Principles**
- Co-created framework referenced throughout the blog posts and book
- Principles most relevant to the book:
  - Principle 1: Our highest priority is to be our best self and enable others to be their best selves
  - Principle 3: Build daily self-care habits
  - Principle 4: Engage your trusted circle daily
  - Principle 7: Investing time in yourself is the primary measure of progress
  - Principle 8: Prioritize being more over doing more
  - Principle 9: Continuous attention to scientific research enhances best self
  - Principle 12: At regular intervals, reflect on how to become your best self, then tune and adjust
- **Status:** Primary source — Brian is co-creator
