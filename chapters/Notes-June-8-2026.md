# Book Notes
# June 8th, 2026 - driving back from Canada fishing trip

Biggest fear I have is to fall short on caring for my wife and family.  I probably have created in my own mind unrealistic expectations for what that actually means.  This is where I hear the inner critic in my own voice in a self-depricating, negative tone.  You fell short.  I should have known and anticipated this or that.  I am not working hard enough.

Most of the time I can lean on the inner advocate to get me through by stepping outside of what the inner critic is saying with a level of curiosity (from learning a little bit how about meditation works) and calling the inner advocate in to figure out what is really true.

When I can't pull off this internal mindset shift, I really do feel bad.  The conversation the inner critic carries out really gets bad sometimes and I feel shame just like Brene Brown describes - I have a pretty typical response as a lot of men, as she shares, of feeling weakness - I fell short and unworthy.

Certain situations require taking that to the next step.  Asking myself what would great look like right now is a great question to move from the noise inside to real problem-solving with rational solutions.

This came from Cy Wakeman's "Reality-based Leadership" - how to diffuse drama in our own heads as well as helping others do the same, thus impacting an organization's culture.  See her TED Talk on YouTube (get the link later when I am online)

Some of this is redundant with other stories and context I am sure.  I am feeling like the question/answer about shame is not entirely completely honest - not intentionally, but now seeing how that is presented in context helped me see a way to weave this in.  If there is a sense this is too much information, let's revisit the whole shame concept.  My purpose for sharing is that I expect that many people wrestle with shame, and my story is probably the same as many others.  To use Brene Brown's terminology, I am showing vulnerability by sharing my story, not knowing for sure how this lands successfully.
