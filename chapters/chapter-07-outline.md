### 4. Behavior Is a Function of Environment

Clear's environment-design concept in action: behavior is a function of environment, not willpower. Life Time as the example.

**Available material (strong):**

**Status: minimal gap.** This section is close to draft-ready as is.

### 5. The Pivot

The creatine story — the strongest material in the chapter.

**Available material (strong):**
- Early InBody scan shows muscle mass and fat dropping together — not the goal
- Kaylee flags it and points to creatine as a possible cause
- Brian is skeptical, takes it to Dr. Lee, asks directly whether creatine is safe
- Dr. Lee confirms — "a resounding yes"
- Creatine added; the next InBody scan (roughly two weeks later) shows the lean-muscle-mass curve reverse
- "That was the moment the inner advocate started showing up regularly. The inner critic got relegated to the back seat — not gone, but quieter."
- Ties into the broader measurement philosophy already captured: "Watch for unintended consequences... A Life Time trainer flagged it, Dr. Lee confirmed it, and creatine was added. Within a month the LMM curve reversed. The system corrected."

**Status: ready.** This section can essentially draft itself from existing material.
