## New Personal Material Worth Gathering

### 1. Chad (Life Time)

Named in outline.md alongside Kaylee as one of the Life Time staff who supported the practice, but he currently has zero individual story anywhere in personal-stories.md. Right now "Input Isn't the Same as Ownership" names Kaylee with a full story (the creatine flag) and Dr. Lee with a full story (interval training, supplement guidance, the safety confirmation), but Chad doesn't appear in the chapter at all because there's nothing to draw on. A specific memory, something Chad said, a piece of advice he gave, a moment he noticed something, would let him be named in the chapter rather than left out entirely.

### 2. Chris, Anna, Sid, and Darcy (3M)

Same gap, at a larger scale. These four are named together in outline.md as the trainers who built Brian's foundational strength-training knowledge at 3M's corporate gym, years before the 2025 journey began. In the current draft they're mentioned as a group ("the trainers I worked with at 3M, Chris, Anna, Sid, and Darcy, built the foundation years before any of this was urgent") but with no individual anecdotes attached to any of them. Even one concrete scene, a specific correction one of them gave, a specific piece of form or programming advice that stuck for a decade, would strengthen both "The System Built for This Body, This Life, This Season" and "Input Isn't the Same as Ownership" considerably, and would make four real people feel like four real people instead of a list of names.

### 3. More on the 2016 attempt

This is currently the thinnest scene in the chapter: two or three sentences in "Borrowed Systems Don't Hold" ("A different doctor gave me a small nudge, nothing urgent, just a suggestion. The approach was simple: work out and eat less. It worked, for a while. I don't remember exactly why it stopped."). By contrast, the 2020 app-and-pandemic attempt now has real texture (the judgmental feedback loops, the calorie pressure, the self-aware "that's the story I told myself" line). If there's a specific moment tied to 2016 worth remembering, a conversation, a feeling when it faded, what the doctor actually said, it would balance the two attempts against each other instead of leaving one much richer than the other.

### 4. A specific Claude/Apple Fitness moment

The post-workout review section currently describes what Claude does for the review in general terms: "keeping track of data points and noticing trends I wouldn't catch on my own without a lot of manual work... flagging new personal bests, and holding my focus on the long game." That's accurate but abstract. One concrete instance, a specific trend Claude caught that Brian wouldn't have noticed himself, a specific personal best it flagged, a specific moment the long-game framing kicked in when a single bad number might have derailed the mood, would make this beat land the way the creatine story lands, with a real scene instead of a description of a capability.

## Research and Citation Opportunities

### 5. Creatine for older adults (Candow et al., 2025)

This is the strongest, lowest-effort opportunity on this list. Candow, D. G., et al., "Creatine Monohydrate Supplementation for Older Adults and Clinical Populations," *Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition*, 22(1), 2025, is already cited in Chapter 3 as endnote 9. It's directly relevant to the creatine pivot in "The Pivot" section of Chapter 7, and that section currently has no citation at all behind Dr. Lee's advice, it's told purely as personal narrative. Reusing endnote 9 in Chapter 7 would back the "at over fifty, the body loses lean muscle mass quickly, and creatine can help reverse that" line with real, already-vetted research, and would create a nice cross-chapter thread (the same citation doing scientific work in Chapter 3 and narrative work in Chapter 7).

### 6. Interval training vs. steady-state cardio

Dr. Lee's advice in "The System Built for This Body, This Life, This Season" ("interval training over straight steady-state work, because the heart rate going up and coming back down does something steady cardio doesn't") is currently presented as personal medical advice with no research behind it, which is fine as personal narrative but is also exactly the kind of claim the writing rules flag for sourcing ("prefer statements of fact to have a verifiable source behind them"). There is real exercise-science literature on interval training and cardiovascular adaptation. If Brian wants this claim to carry more than anecdotal weight, this would need a search for a specific, citable study before it goes in sources.md and gets an endnote.

### 7. Protein timing after resistance training

The spring 2026 refinement in "The System Built for This Body, This Life, This Season" (protein shortly after lifting on strength training days, "to help the muscle actually keep and grow what the training built") touches on muscle protein synthesis and what's popularly called the "anabolic window." There's real sports-nutrition research behind post-workout protein timing. As with #6, this would need a specific study identified and logged in sources.md before citing, but it would give this detail more authority than "this is what I do."

### 8. Tighten the Clear (*Atomic Habits*) citation

The current endnote 16 citation in "Make It Easier Than Not Doing It" is a generic paraphrase of Clear's environment-design idea, not a specific quote or page reference. Per the existing sourcing rule ("if a statistic or claim appears in the text, it must exist in sources.md with a verifiable citation"), a specific line from *Atomic Habits* on environment design, with a page number, would tighten this rather than leaving it as an unattributed paraphrase of a real author's core idea.

## Structural and Reflective Expansion (No New Material Needed)

### 9. Connect environment design to nutrition, not just the gym

"Make It Easier Than Not Doing It" currently applies Clear's environment-design principle only to the Life Time gym. The eating window material in Section 2 (delayed breakfast, no eating after dinner, protein timing) is itself an environment-design choice, arguably a stronger one, since it's about structuring time and access rather than a physical space. A short passage connecting the two would tie Sections 2 and 4 together and reinforce the chapter's central idea instead of leaving it illustrated by only one example.

### 10. A more explicit bridge back to hope at the chapter's close

"Build Yours" currently ends on autonomy and ownership language ("The system that will actually hold you through a hard season is the one you build, piece by piece, out of what you learn about your own body and what it actually needs.") without explicitly reconnecting to the hope practice itself. Chapter 6's closer named the connection to hope directly. A sentence or two doing the same here would keep the book's throughline consistent and satisfy the "always bring it back to hope explicitly" principle from writing-rules.md.

### 11. A brief forward gesture to Chapter 8 inside the body text

Right now, the connection to Chapter 8 (measurement, reading the receipt, AI as a retrospective partner) only exists in the Chapter Notes footer, which readers never see. Chapter 6 lightly foreshadowed later chapters (Chapter 8, Chapter 12) within the actual prose. A similarly light touch near the end of "The Pivot" or "Build Yours," something like a single sentence gesturing toward "knowing the plan is working" as the next question, would create the same kind of forward pull for readers without duplicating Chapter 8's content.

### 12. A dedicated reflection on "the inner advocate starting to win"

Outline.md names this as one of the throughlines for Chapter 7 specifically ("The inner advocate starting to win"), but in the current draft it only appears explicitly inside "The Pivot," tied to the creatine story. A short reflective passage, either standalone or folded into "Build Yours," on how the inner advocate's growing presence shows up across the whole system-building process (choosing to build custom from day one, trusting Kaylee and Dr. Lee, renaming the eating window, correcting course twice) would make this an explicit thread running through the chapter rather than a single moment inside one section.
