## Introduction: The Word I Didn't Know I Needed

The story of Epiphany Sunday 2025. A word arrives — hope — and it feels like a gift. A mother declining. A doctor's visit that makes things very clear. A 60th birthday approaching. The decision that becomes a journey. And the discovery that changes everything: hope is not a feeling. It's a practice worth building.

Sets up the thesis, the tone, and the invitation.

---

## Part One: Why Hope Is Not What You Think

### Chapter 1: The Lie We Tell Ourselves About Hope
Hope as passive waiting. The common misconception — hope as mood, disposition, something the optimists have and the rest of us wait for. Why that framing keeps people stuck. The distinction between hope and optimism. Introduction of C.R. Snyder's Hope Theory — agency and pathways. Hope as cognitive, not emotional. Hope as something you do.

*Story woven in:* The years of knowing what to do and not doing it. The inner critic winning too many mornings in a row.

### Chapter 2: What Hope Actually Is
A deeper exploration of the framework. Agency — the belief that you can move forward. Pathways — the ability to find or create a route when the obvious one is blocked. Why hopeful people are more clear-eyed about obstacles than optimists. The Hope Scale. Hope as a verb.

*Story woven in:* The bad doctor's visit. The moment things became clear. The decision to give myself a gift.

### Chapter 3: The Foundation You're Already Standing On
The role of prior work in making the hope practice possible. Agile Best Self as a jumping off point — the 12 principles as scaffolding. What it means to already have a framework for self-reflection and growth when a crisis arrives. You don't build a hope practice from scratch — you build it from where you are.

*Story woven in:* The Agile Best Self journey. Co-creating a framework with Michaele Gardner. Jesse Fewell's *Untapped Agility* — Chapter 7.

---

## Part Two: Building Your Hope Practice

### Chapter 4: Find Your Keyword
The anchor mechanism. Why a single word can cut through noise and reconnect you to your deepest why in seconds. How "Pictures" became the reset trigger. The legacy frame — what story will your presence tell across generations? How to find your own keyword. What makes a keyword work.

*Story woven in:* The keyword "Pictures." 5am alarms. The moments the word saved the morning.

### Chapter 5: The Wrong Metric
Why what you measure matters as much as what you do. The scale as a false signal. The shift from weight to lean muscle mass. How measuring the wrong thing can undermine a hope practice that is actually working. What to measure instead — and how to know when you've found the right signal.

*Story woven in:* The plateau. The curiosity. The shift that changed everything.

### Chapter 6: You Can't Build It Alone
The trusted circle — not formal accountability, but the right people with the right expertise at the right time. The doctor. The gym community. The quiet, non-controlling support of a partner on her own journey. What it means to engage your circle in service of your hope practice. The difference between emotional support and expert guidance.

*Story woven in:* Building the team. The gym as community. A wife who believed without controlling.

### Chapter 7: The Two Sides of the Practice
Building a hope practice isn't just about what you add — it's about what you protect against. Intentional reduction as protection, not deprivation. Alcohol. Sugar. The things that quietly erode what you're building. Recovery as part of the design. Sleep. Rest. Intentional rewards woven close to the behavior. Sustainability as a principle, not an afterthought.

*Story woven in:* The intentional reductions. The hot tub. The new workout shorts. Learning what Principle 8 means in practice.

### Chapter 8: The Ritual That Found Me
The dumbbell milestone ritual. Picking up each weight corresponding to lbs lost. Feeling in your hands what your body was carrying every single moment of every single day. How a small act becomes a ceremony. Why this retrospective got more powerful over time — the opposite of most retros. The double meaning of the weight you were carrying.

*Story woven in:* The full dumbbell journey — 10 through 85 lbs. The photos. The Life Time gym. The 85 lb dumbbell you can barely lift with one arm.

### Chapter 9: Hacking Your Own System
The iterative approach. Running experiments on yourself. Paying attention to what the data is telling you — especially when it surprises you. The post-practice review. Could I come back tomorrow? Learning from every iteration, not just the big ones. Becoming a student of your own system.

*Story woven in:* The year as a series of experiments. What worked. What didn't. What each failure taught.

---

## Part Three: What the Practice Makes Possible

### Chapter 10: Hope Has a Target
Hope is not vague aspiration — it needs something specific to aim at. The Pictures concept — future moments you are actively moving toward. Graduations. Weddings. Grandchildren. Breaking 80 consistently. Walking 18 holes. Arriving at retirement in the best shape of your recent life. How specific targets feed the practice and the practice feeds the targets.

*Story woven in:* The golf journey — 50 years, competitive roots, rededication. Breaking 80 as a retirement goal. The Orlando trip. The ride with the harness that will finally close.

### Chapter 11: Hope Forward
Hoping across generations. Legacy as a hope practice. What the photographs will say long after you are gone. The father lost to Alzheimer's. The mother who gave you the word on Epiphany Sunday and then left. What it means to be the family steward. What you are building for the people who come after you.

*Story woven in:* Parents' passing. The oldest of four children. The weight of stewardship. The gift of the word "hope."

### Chapter 12: The Practice Never Ends
Hope practices mature and evolve. The milestones space out. The wins get quieter. The foundation gets deeper. What the practice looks like in a new season — post-surgery recovery, a body still changing, a game still improving. Reflect, tune, adjust. The invitation to keep going.

*Story woven in:* Post-surgery recovery. The hernia. Back on the treadmill by Day 2. Running by Day 13. The ongoing journey.

---

## Conclusion: Build Yours

The invitation. This is not a prescription — it is a personal framework and a generous offer. Here is what I learned. Here are the principles underneath it. Now go find what works for you. Your keyword. Your metric. Your circle. Your ritual. Your pictures.

Hope is not a feeling. It's a practice worth building. And it starts wherever you are.
