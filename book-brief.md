# The Hope Habit - Book Brief

## Working Title
**The Hope Habit: Small Steps That Change Everything**

## Author
Brian Hackerson

## Core Thesis
Hope is not a feeling — it is something you do. It is a practice, a habit, a series of small actions taken consistently in the direction of something that matters deeply to you. Hope requires action.

## Author's Definition of Hope
*"Hope is the feeling that comes when self-discipline meets adversity and wins."*
— Brian Hackerson

## Origin Story
On Epiphany Sunday 2025, Brian received the word "hope" at church — a moment that felt like a gift from his recently declining mother. He has been building on it ever since, through one of the most challenging and meaningful years of his life.

## Primary Audience
- General public / personal growth readers
- People going through difficult life transitions

## Tone
Inspirational and motivational with a research backbone. Personal stories illustrate concepts but the focus is on the reader's journey, not the author's memoir.

## Key Source Material
- C.R. Snyder's Hope Theory (agency + pathways)
- Agile Best Self framework and 12 principles (co-created by Brian Hackerson and Michaele Gardner)
- New Testament scripture (Lutheran tradition)
- Brian's personal stories from 2025

## Key Concepts
- Hope as a verb, not a feeling
- The keyword mechanism — finding your personal anchor word
- Agency (the will to move forward) and pathways (finding the route)
- Legacy and hoping forward across generations
- The "Pictures" concept — future moments worth living for
- Small steps as the engine of sustainable hope
- Community — like-hearted people as hope amplifiers

## Agile Best Self Principles Most Relevant to the Book
- Principle 1: Our highest priority is to be our best self and enable others to be their best selves
- Principle 3: Build daily self-care habits
- Principle 7: Investing time in yourself is the primary measure of progress
- Principle 12: At regular intervals, reflect on how to become your best self, then tune and adjust
