# Connecting the concepts of Joy to Hope
# June 8, 2026

Consistency in building and expanding the hope practice creates great conditions for joy to happen in life

Example from the fishing trip

improved body, improved mindset
Felt a next-level kind of joy that comes with fellowship with my friends, physically moved through the experience with ease, willingness to try new things, creating new pictures that are receipts.

When we got off the lake both days, we downloaded and shared photos to a shared cloud drive.  One particular picture caught my eye.  It was the pose of me with a big walleye I had the good fortune to get into the boat.  My new shape was really accentuated and the inner advocate was quick to pick that up and run with it.  I had access to shared drives from past trips and I looked through them and found pictures of me in the same pose in June of 2023, and June 2024.  The contrast was striking.  A leaner face, defined jaw line (the 2023 and 2024 photos make my face look much rounder), the body has changed visibly.  I felt proud of what had been accomplished.

The receipts are not something that warrant recognition or celebration from others , but are a reminder and reinforcement of who I want to be and that the hope practice is not over yet.  The work continues.

The other thing I noticed is how much more I enjoyed the whole experience.  Less friction meant I was more present with my friends and enjoyed the fellowship because I was not too tired.  Enjoyed every bite of shore lunch I ate.  Enjoyed every fish I was able to catch.  Enjoyed celebrating with others when they caught a big walleye.  Enjoyed every moment of sunshine and light breeze. Sounds like joy to me.

Also joy knowing we have a reservation for next year.  Same time, same friends, same resort.

Joy - need to define it
