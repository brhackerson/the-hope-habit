## Status

## Core Idea

There is a difference between quitting and pivoting. Quitting walks away from the practice. Pivoting changes direction within it. The hope practice is not a fixed destination — it is a living system that responds to evidence.

The receipt matters here. A pivot is not a feeling or a mood. It is a data-driven decision. You read the receipts, assess what they tell you, and adjust course when the original goal no longer serves the journey.

## Personal Anchor (background — not for publication)

May 21, 2026: Following an InBody scan showing meaningful progress, Brian recalibrated his primary fitness goal. The original target was arbitrary and had served its purpose. The new goal is leaner and more specific. The muscle base is built. The fat loss phase begins.

**Key distinction for the post:** This was not quitting. The practice did not change. The training load did not change. The goal changed — because the receipts said it was time.

## The Universal Question

How do you know when a goal has served its purpose?
What does it look like to change direction without quitting?
What separates a pivot from giving up?

---

## Draft Outline

1. The pivot is not the same as quitting — define the distinction
2. What earns the right to pivot — the receipt (data, evidence, honest assessment)
3. How the pivot happens — read the receipts, assess the goal, set new intention, act
4. The ABS connection — Principle 12: reflect, tune, adjust. The pivot is Principle 12 in action.
5. The hope connection — pivoting keeps hope alive. Staying locked to a goal that no longer serves you drains it.
6. Invitation to the reader — what goal are you carrying that may have already served its purpose?

---

## ABS Connection

Principle 12: At regular intervals, reflect on how to become your best self, then tune and adjust behavior to be in alignment.

The pivot is not a failure of commitment. It is the practice working exactly as designed.

## Research to Consider

- Snyder's pathways dimension — when one route is blocked or completed, finding a new route is hope in action, not retreat
- Joiner's Reflective Capacity cycle (from Fewell, Chapter 7) — assess, diagnose, set intentions, act, reassess. The pivot lives in step 1 and 3.
